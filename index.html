How to Use

  1. Enter the pressure and select its unit (bars, kbars, GPa, or kPa).
  2. Enter the density of the substrate and select its unit (g/cm³ or kg/cm³).
  3. Choose a gravitational acceleration from the preset planetary values, or select "Custom" to enter your own.
  4. Click Calculate Depth (or press Enter) to compute the depth.

Formula

Depth is calculated from the hydrostatic pressure equation:

h = P / (ρ · g)

Where:

  • h = depth (m)
  • P = pressure (Pa)
  • ρ = density (kg/m³)
  • g = gravitational acceleration (m/s²)

All inputs are converted to SI units before calculation.

Unit Conversions

Pressure

  • 1 bar = 105 Pa
  • 1 kbar = 108 Pa
  • 1 GPa = 109 Pa
  • 1 kPa = 103 Pa

Density

  • 1 g/cm³ = 1,000 kg/m³
  • 1 kg/cm³ = 106 kg/m³

Gravitational Acceleration Values

Surface gravity values used in this calculator:

  • Earth: 9.807 m/s²
  • Mars: 3.711 m/s²
  • Moon: 1.62 m/s²
  • Mercury: 3.7 m/s²
  • Venus: 8.87 m/s²
  • Jupiter: 24.79 m/s²
  • Saturn: 10.44 m/s²
  • Uranus: 8.69 m/s²
  • Neptune: 11.15 m/s²

About

This calculator computes the depth within a planetary substrate given a pressure and bulk density, assuming a uniform-density column under hydrostatic equilibrium. It is intended as a quick estimation tool for petrological and geophysical applications.
